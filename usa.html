How the USA is being affected by Climate Change:

  1. Warmer temperature will increase due to the climate change that is occurring in the United States of America. Additionally, these warmer temperatures will increase the frequency, intensity, and duration of heat waves which are very detrimental to human and animal health, particularly for young children and the elderly.

  2. Climate change can also impact human health by worsening air and water quality which would increase the spread of fatal disease, altering the frequency or intensity of extreme weather events.

  3. Climate change will also foster the growth of sea levels, rising them by a tremendous amount. These unexpected rising sea levels will threaten costal communities and ecosystems.

  4. Climate change will also alter the patterns and the amounts of rainfaill due to more water evaporating because of the global temberatures also rising. This will change the timing and amount of stream flow, affecting water supplies and water quality and the production of hydroelectricity.

  5. Climate change will also influence geographic ranges of many plant and animal species. Meaning, the timing of their life cycle events, such as migration and reproduction will be latered.

  6. Finally, there will be frequent occurrencee of extreme weather events such as heat waves, droughts, and floods. This will increase losses to property which will result in disruptions to society and reduce the affordability of insurance for many individuals all across the nation.

How the USA contributes to climate change:

In the first graph, it shows the emission values different activities produce in the United States of America. However, for a more general answer, human activites are responsible for almost all of the increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere over the last 150 eyars. Moreover, the largest source of greenhouse gas emissions from human activities in the United States of America is from burning fossil fuels for heat, transportation, and electricity.

The Environmental policies set in place by USA Government:

According to the White House (whitehouse.gov), they are planning the folllwing things:

  1. Reduce U.S. greenhouse gas emissions 50-52% below 2005 levels in 2030.

  2. Reach 100% carbon pollution-free electricity by 2035.

  3. Achieve a net-zero emissions economy by 2050.

  4. Deliver 40% of the benefits from federal investments in climate and clean energy to disadvantages communities.
